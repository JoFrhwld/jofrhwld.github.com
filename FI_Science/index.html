More Info

Since 1972, groups of students at UPenn have been interviewing Philadelphians about their lives and neighborhoods. It's the longest running fieldwork project of its kind, connected to a course taught by William Labov (2013 Benjamin Franklin Medal Laureate) called "The Study of the Speech Community."

We have been using this data to try to figure out the answers to questions like:

  • What is distinctive about the Philadelphia dialect?
  • What can Philadelphia tell us about all speech communities?
  • How do social factors, like your age, education, and gender affect the way you speak?
  • What factors influence your choices when there is more than one way to say the same thing?
  • What are the cognitive factors involved when language changes?

We've discovered many interesting things, like

  • Philadelphia has had many dialectal features in common with the American South, but that's been changing.
  • When language is changing, women tend to be about a generation in advance of men.
  • Even apparently mundane variation in speech is influenced in complex ways by cognitive and social forces.
